# Legal and safety baseline for Japan

This is an engineering baseline, not legal advice.

## Telephone solicitation

For a sales solicitation call, Consumer Affairs Agency guidance under Japan's Specified Commercial
Transactions framework requires disclosure, before solicitation, of the business name, caller name,
type of goods or service, and solicitation purpose. A clear refusal must be respected; continued or
repeated solicitation is prohibited. The opening prompt and permanent suppression callback implement
that minimum behavior.

## Personal information and recordings

A recording that can identify a person may be personal information, and the utilization purpose must
be notified or published. The Personal Information Protection Commission FAQ explains there is not a
universal APPI requirement to announce recording in every case, but advance notice is the safer
operational default. Voiceprints used to identify people require additional care.

## Repository safeguards

- No unsolicited bulk-call endpoint
- Required consent basis
- Weekday business-hour enforcement
- Daily quota and per-recipient cooldown
- Permanent suppression list
- AI disclosure and permission-to-continue opening
- Human transfer for sensitive or high-stakes matters
- Recording disabled by default

Before launch, obtain advice tailored to the product, customer relationship, script, recording, data
location, carrier terms, and every target jurisdiction.
